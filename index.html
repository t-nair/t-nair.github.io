About Me

I'm Tanya!

I’m a Computer Engineering student specializing in artificial intelligence and machine learning. My past experiences lie in the field of Natural Language Processing, and I hope to continue to deepen my knowledge of this field and broaden the spectrum of models I build in future projects.

My key research interests: RAG/Agentic AI, improving efficiency of AI systems, PIML

Research & Projects

RAG for Pathology Reports - Levy Lab (Dartmouth Hitchcock Health Center)

Worked under the guidance of Dr. Joshua Levy. Developed a retrieval-augmented generation model using a fusion search method incorporating Okapi BM-25 and FAISS for clinician cross-referencing of pathology reports.

Additional Info

Advancing Accessibility for Engineering Education - University of Washington Seattle

Worked under the guidance of Prof. Anant P. Anantram and Dr. Lalitha Subramanian. Created a novel pipeline to mass-enhance the accessibility of PPT presentations for blind/low-vision students pursuing higher education.

View Details

[Paper]: Natural Language Processing used in sentiment analysis of poetry: a study of six common techniques

Conducted a comparative study of six different natural language processing techniques when applied to sentiment analysis of English poetry. Published in the Journal of Student Research: High School Edition in May 2023.
